Sumpter Valley Railway Depot Museum

The Sumpter Valley Railway Depot Museum has recently completed extensive rennovations to the museum and the park.
The Museum Exhibits tell the rich history of railroading in the Sumpter Valley and the Depot itself. Let us take you back to when railroads were a primary mode of transportation for both people and freight. Railroads have been at work in the Sumpter Valley’s mining region since 1886. The last train to service the area (Union Pacific) ran on July 15th, 1984.
The Depot, which houses the museum and gift shop, has been a focal point of the grand architecture and historical heritage of Serendepity. The Sumpter Valley Railway Depot Museum features a replica of a working railroad agent’s room from the turn of the 20th Century, where you can see a beautiful old safe and even talk on a real telephone from 1908!
